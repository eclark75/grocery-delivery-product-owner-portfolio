# Grocery Delivery App Case Study

## Project Overview

The Grocery Delivery App is a Product Owner portfolio project demonstrating Agile product management skills. The objective was to design a simple grocery ordering experience that allows customers to browse products, add items to a cart, and complete purchases quickly.

---

## Problem Statement

Many customers have limited time to shop for groceries in person. Existing grocery applications can be confusing, slow, or require too many checkout steps.

The goal was to create a simple and intuitive shopping experience that reduces friction throughout the ordering process.

---

## Product Vision

Deliver a fast, reliable, and user-friendly grocery delivery experience that helps customers shop from anywhere with confidence.

---

## Target Users

- Busy professionals
- Parents with families
- Senior citizens
- Customers with limited mobility

---

## Business Goals

- Increase online grocery orders
- Improve customer satisfaction
- Reduce cart abandonment
- Increase repeat purchases

---

## My Role

As Product Owner, I was responsible for:

- Creating the product vision
- Writing user stories
- Prioritizing the product backlog
- Defining acceptance criteria
- Planning sprint goals
- Creating the product roadmap
- Collaborating with stakeholders
- Designing wireframes

---

## Agile Process

The project followed Scrum practices by:

- Creating user personas
- Building a prioritized backlog
- Planning Sprint 1
- Defining a Minimum Viable Product (MVP)
- Conducting sprint reviews
- Performing sprint retrospectives

---

## Outcome

The resulting portfolio demonstrates Product Owner responsibilities including:

- Product Vision
- User Story Creation
- Product Backlog Management
- Sprint Planning
- Product Roadmapping
- Stakeholder Thinking
- User-Centered Design
